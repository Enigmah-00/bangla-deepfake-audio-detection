## 1. About the Event

SciBlitz AI Challenge is the flagship AI competition of SciBlitz, the national technology festival organized by the IEEE Student Branch of Chittagong University of Engineering & Technology (CUET). The competition invites student teams from across Bangladesh to design and build AI-powered products that address real-world problems.

The event follows a two-stage format:

- **Stage 1 (Remote):** Teams independently build their projects and submit a complete project package by July 8, 2026.
- **Stage 2 (Final Day):** Shortlisted teams present their work live at CUET on July 17, 2026, in front of a panel of judges.

## 3. Eligibility

### 3.1 Who Can Participate

- The competition is open to all currently enrolled students at any university, college, or polytechnic institute in Bangladesh.
- Teams may include members from different institutions.
- Students from any discipline are welcome; you do not need to be from a computer science or engineering background.
- Faculty members and non-students are not permitted to be team members. They may serve as mentors only.

### 3.2 Team Criteria

- Each team must have a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 4 members.
- A participant may only be a member of one team. Dual registration will result in the disqualification of both teams.
- Cross-university teams are allowed.
- One member of the team must be designated as the **Team Lead**. All official communication will go through the Team Lead.

## 5. Project Requirements

### 5.1 Core Requirement

Artificial intelligence or machine learning must be a central and meaningful component of the project, not a peripheral feature.

A project that merely calls an API without meaningful integration, customization, or application to a problem will not be considered compliant.

### 5.2 Originality & Development Window

- The project must be original work as defined by this rulebook.
- The GitHub repository must show commits made during the competition window: **May 14–July 8, 2026**.
- Repositories with no commits during this period will be flagged.

### 5.3 Permitted Tools & Technologies

- Any programming language, framework, or cloud platform is allowed.
- The use of pre-trained models, foundation models such as GPT-4, Gemini, and LLaMA, and fine-tuned models is fully permitted and encouraged.
- Public APIs such as OpenAI, Hugging Face, and Google AI are allowed. Usage costs are the team's responsibility.
- Open-source libraries and datasets are allowed. All third-party resources must be properly attributed.
- No-code or low-code AI tools, such as Bubble or FlutterFlow with AI plugins, are permitted for **Track D only**, provided that:
  - the report clearly explains the AI logic; and
  - the team can answer technical questions during judging.

### 5.4 Live Demo Requirement

The project must be deployed and accessible through a public URL. Judges will evaluate the product directly in the browser.

No local setup, installation, or login should be required to access the core demo.

Accepted hosting platforms include, but are not limited to:

- **Hugging Face Spaces** — recommended for ML, Gradio, or Streamlit applications
- **Streamlit Community Cloud**
- **Vercel or Netlify** — for frontend-heavy applications
- **Render** — for full-stack applications with a backend

> **Important:** The hosted application must remain live and responsive until at least **July 12, 2026**. Test the application on the morning of July 9 to ensure that it is not sleeping or inactive. Applications that are down or unresponsive during judging will not receive additional time.

If the project is genuinely not a web application—for example, an embedded system, mobile application, or hardware integration—the team must contact the organizing committee before July 8 to request approval for an alternative demonstration format.

Approval is not guaranteed.

## 7. Submission Requirements

All deliverables must be submitted through the official submission form by **July 8, 2026, at 11:59 PM BST**.

Incomplete submissions will be disqualified.

### 7.1 Required Deliverables

#### 1. Live Public URL

The project must be deployed and accessible through a public URL. Judges will evaluate the product directly in the browser.

No local setup, installation, or login should be required to access the core demo.

If the project is genuinely not a web application—for example, an embedded system, mobile application, or hardware integration—the team must contact the organizing committee before July 8 to request approval for an alternative demonstration format.

Approval is not guaranteed.

#### 2. Project Report — PDF, Maximum 8 Pages

The report must cover:

1. Problem statement
2. Proposed solution
3. Methodology
4. AI/ML approach
5. Results
6. Limitations and future work

Additional requirements:

- Minimum font size: **10 pt**
- Figures and tables are encouraged

#### 3. GitHub Repository

The repository must:

- be public by the submission deadline;
- contain a clear `README.md`;
- describe the project;
- list the technology stack;
- include setup instructions; and
- show development activity through commits dated between **May 14 and July 8, 2026**.

#### 4. Demo Video

Submit a **3–5 minute** screen recording that demonstrates the working product.

The video must be hosted on:

- YouTube as an unlisted video; or
- Google Drive with link sharing enabled.

> This is an important submission artifact. Judges will watch it first.

#### 5. Model & Data Card

Submit a single-page document covering:

- the dataset or datasets used;
- the source of each dataset;
- any pre-trained model used;
- the model name;
- the model provider;
- the applicable license; and
- any known limitations or ethical concerns related to the AI component.

### 7.2 Submission Format Summary

| Deliverable | Format | Submission Method |
|---|---|---|
| Live Public URL | URL | Paste the link into the submission form |
| Project Report | PDF | Upload through the submission form |
| GitHub Repository | Public URL | Paste the link into the form |
| Demo Video | YouTube or Google Drive link | Paste the link into the form |
| Model & Data Card | PDF, 1 page | Upload through the submission form |

## 8. Judging Criteria

All submissions will be evaluated by a panel of at least two judges.

Scores from all judges will be averaged. The organizing committee may moderate scores in the event of significant discrepancies.

| Criterion | Weight | Maximum Score |
|---|---:|---:|
| Innovation & Originality | 25% | 25 |
| Technical Implementation | 25% | 25 |
| Real-World Impact & Relevance | 20% | 20 |
| Demo Quality & Functionality | 20% | 20 |
| Presentation & Communication | 10% | 10 |
| **Total** | **100%** | **100** |

### 8.1 Evaluation Notes

- **Technical Implementation** does not require training a model from scratch. Effective, appropriate, and well-integrated use of existing tools and APIs can score well.
- **Demo Quality** is evaluated based on the live hosted URL and the demo video. A broken demo will result in a significantly lower score for this criterion.
- **Innovation** is assessed relative to the problem context. A novel approach to a local problem may score higher than a technically complex but routine solution.
- The **Presentation** score applies only to teams participating in the Final Day.
- For remote-stage teams, communication scores are based on the report and demo video.

### 8.2 Shortlisting

After the submission deadline, the **top 30 teams** by aggregate score will be shortlisted for the Final Day.

Shortlisted teams will be notified by **July 10, 2026**.

The shortlisting decision is final.

## 9. Final Day — July 17, 2026

### 9.1 Format

- Each shortlisted team will receive **5 minutes** to present.
- The presentation will be followed by **3 minutes of questions and answers** from the judges.
- Teams may demonstrate their live product during the presentation slot.

### 9.2 Attendance

- At least one team member must be physically present at the venue on July 17.
- Remaining members may participate remotely by video call, subject to venue connectivity, but the organizing committee must be informed in advance.
- Teams must be available and ready for the full Final Day schedule.
- The presentation order will be communicated to shortlisted teams on July 16.
- Failure to present at the assigned time without prior notice will result in disqualification from the final.

### 9.3 Technical Preparation

- Check the live demo URL on the evening of July 16.
- Some platforms, such as Hugging Face Spaces on a free tier, may sleep after inactivity. Visit the application to wake it up.
- Bring a backup copy of the demo video in case the live demo fails.
- Bring the presentation slides on a USB drive as a backup.

## 10. Rules & Conduct

### 10.1 Prohibited Actions

The following actions are prohibited:

- Plagiarism of code, ideas, text, or visual content without proper attribution
- Using another team's work
- Having a non-team member contribute to the project
- Misrepresenting the capabilities of the product in the demo video or presentation
- Registering as a member of more than one team
- Harassment, disrespect, or disruptive conduct toward other participants, judges, or organizers, whether online or in person
- Attempting to contact or influence judges regarding scoring

### 10.2 Attribution & Open Source

- All third-party code, models, datasets, and libraries used must be listed in the `README` and the Model & Data Card.
- The use of open-source code is permitted, provided that the applicable license is respected and attribution is given.
- Violation of a third-party license is grounds for disqualification.

### 10.3 Intellectual Property

- Teams retain full ownership of their projects and all intellectual property created during the competition.
- By registering, teams grant IEEE Student Branch CUET a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to feature their project name, description, and demo screenshots in SciBlitz promotional and documentation materials.
- IEEE Student Branch CUET will not commercially use or redistribute any team's codebase without explicit written permission.

### 10.4 Disqualification

The organizing committee reserves the right to disqualify any team found to be in violation of these rules at any stage of the competition.

Disqualification decisions are final.

If a team is disqualified after paying the registration fee, the registration fee is non-refundable.
